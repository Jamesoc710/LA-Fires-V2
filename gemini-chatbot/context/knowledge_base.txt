LA FIRES REBUILDING KNOWLEDGE BASE (Eaton Fire / Palisades Fire, Jan 2025)
Current as of July 2026, about 18 months after the fires.

A second County center covers unincorporated communities hit by the Palisades Fire (e.g. Topanga): 27001 Agoura Road, Suite 250, Calabasas, CA 91302, (818) 880-4150, walk-ins Mon-Fri 7-11am. ## PERMIT PROCESS BASICS

Streamlined "like-for-like" rebuilding is still active as of mid-2026, under Gov. Newsom's Executive Order N-4-25 (Jan 2025) and later orders that reaffirmed/adjusted it. Orders are reissued periodically - confirm the current version at gov.ca.gov.

"Like-for-like" generally means rebuilding in the same location, same use, and close to the same size. LA County allows up to a 10% increase in floor area, height, or footprint (200 sq ft in the Eaton Fire area, if greater). The City of LA allows up to 110% of prior footprint and height under the Mayor's Emergency Executive Order 1 (EO 1). Qualifying projects skip discretionary zoning review but must still meet building, fire, and health/safety code; CEQA and Coastal Act review are also suspended for them - confirm current status locally.

Statewide, AB 253 (in effect since January 1, 2026) lets you hire a licensed private plan-check professional if your city or county hasn't completed a residential plan check within 30 business days.

LA County also runs a Building Plan Self-Certification Pilot for unincorporated areas: a licensed CA architect/engineer with 3+ years of experience and required insurance can self-certify single-family, garage, or ADU plans, skipping full plan check (excludes geologically sensitive areas); contact selfcert@pw.lacounty.gov. The City of LA has a separate Standard Plan Pilot: pre-approved home designs usable without a full custom plan check or extra fees, for owners who owned their home by January 7, 2025.

Under EO 1, streamlined-path permits must be obtained by a set cutoff (recently Jan 13, 2032), with three years after issuance to finish construction. Confirm the current deadline at planning.lacity.gov or dbs.lacity.gov.

## DEBRIS REMOVAL STATUS

Both federal debris phases are long finished. Phase 1 (EPA/state hazardous-materials removal) wrapped up late February 2025. Phase 2 (Army Corps ash/structural debris removal) reached substantial completion in 2025, with the last hard-to-access properties finished in early 2026. Owners who opted out used their own licensed contractor.

Before a building permit, you need proof debris removal is done: a Final Sign-Off from the USACE database (government program), or a Final Fire Debris Removal Permit Closure via EPIC-LA (local/private program; or contact FireDebrisPermit@dpw.lacounty.gov). Reusing an existing foundation also needs inspection and approval from County or City Building and Safety.

Soil testing isn't required by FEMA or the Army Corps after cleanup, and no agency mandates it before you rebuild. LA County Public Health and EPA have run voluntary supplemental soil sampling in some areas, but it isn't universal; you can arrange private testing at your own cost - check recovery.lacounty.gov for current programs.

## KEY DEADLINES AND TIME LIMITS (as of July 2026)

FEMA Individual Assistance registration closed March 31, 2025, and will not reopen for this disaster (historical only); for a documented extenuating reason you may still file a late claim via the FEMA Helpline, 1-800-621-3362. SBA disaster loan disbursement deadlines have been extended multiple times, most recently mid-2026 - confirm your current drawdown deadline with SBA (sba.gov).

Rebuild permit deadlines: see streamlined-path cutoffs under Permit Process Basics above; LA County's terms are at recovery.lacounty.gov/rebuilding/like-for-like-rebuild/.

Insurance ALE (additional living expense): if your home is uninhabitable from a state-declared disaster, CA law requires your insurer to cover ALE for at least 24 months, extendable to 36 months, plus a possible further 6-month extension for good cause tied to rebuilding delays outside your control. Insurers must advance at least four months of ALE. Request extensions in writing.

Property tax relief has two tracks. Misfortune & Calamity reassessment: file with the LA County Assessor within 12 months of the damage date (loss over $10,000) for an immediate, temporary reduction. Keeping your old tax base when you rebuild: a "substantially equivalent" rebuild (similar size/use, generally under ~120% of pre-fire value) keeps your prior factored base-year value under Revenue and Taxation Code sections 70/70.5, instead of new-construction reassessment. The normal 5-year rebuild window is extended 3 more years (up to 8 total) for 2024-2025 LA/Ventura fire properties.

Separately, Prop 19 lets disaster victims move their taxable value to a different replacement home instead of rebuilding in place - generally purchased/built within 2 years of selling the original, claimed within 3 years, with over 50% value loss on the original; the replacement can be anywhere in California. You can use the rebuild exclusion or the Prop 19 transfer, not both. Confirm thresholds at assessor.lacounty.gov or boe.ca.gov.

## SB 9 AND ADUs AS REBUILD OPTIONS

SB 9, the statewide lot-split/duplex law, generally still applies in unincorporated Altadena, though it has been more restricted in some Palisades fire-affected areas - check current status with your local planning department. An ADU can get its own certificate of occupancy separately from the main house, so some survivors build a smaller ADU first as interim housing while the main rebuild continues; fire-damaged properties often get priority ADU processing and reduced fees.

## INSURANCE DOCUMENTATION POINTERS

Keep a written, photo, or video inventory of lost belongings; CDI's free Home Inventory Guide (insurance.ca.gov) can help. If you can't itemize everything, California law lets total-loss wildfire survivors request at least 60% of their contents coverage limit upfront without a detailed list. For ALE, save every receipt for temporary housing, meals, storage, and related costs, with dates.

If you can't get standard coverage, the California FAIR Plan is the state's insurer of last resort, with residential coverage up to $3 million; apply through a licensed agent/broker or call 800-339-4099.

For a stuck or disputed claim, contact the CDI Consumer Hotline, 1-800-927-4357 (TTY 800-482-4833), or file a complaint at insurance.ca.gov.
